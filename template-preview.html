Template Preview — Sample Case Study

Overview

Our client is one of Southeast Asia's leading universal banks, operating across eight markets with over 12,000 employees and a strong presence in retail, commercial and private banking. Following a group-wide transformation programme, the incumbent CHRO retired, and the Board required a successor who could sustain cultural change momentum while managing a demanding regulatory environment and an increasingly distributed workforce.

✦ Overview — what to write

Describe who the client is and what the challenge or situation was that led them to engage Whitewater. Write in the third person. Keep it to 3–6 sentences.

Include: type of organisation, scale (geography, headcount, sector), and the business context that drove the mandate. Do not name the client.

Avoid vague openers like "Our client needed help with…" — be specific about the situation.

Solutions Applied

  • Conducted a detailed role specification process with the Board and Group CEO, identifying both technical requirements and cultural fit criteria aligned to the transformation agenda
  • Developed a long-list of 40 candidates across Southeast Asia, Greater China and Australia through direct research and proprietary network activation
  • Delivered structured behavioural competency assessments for six short-listed candidates, with individual written profiles provided to the selection panel
  • Facilitated a two-stage panel interview process, including a board presentation exercise on 100-day strategic priorities
  • Managed offer negotiation and candidate communication through to acceptance and onboarding
✦ Solutions Applied — what to write

List the specific activities and methods Whitewater used to address the client's challenge. Write as action bullets starting with a verb (Conducted, Developed, Facilitated, Designed, etc.).

Aim for 4–6 bullets. Be concrete — "Developed a long-list of 40 candidates" is better than "Identified candidates." Avoid generic consulting language like "Leveraged best-practice frameworks."

For Consulting mandates: describe what was analysed, designed, facilitated or delivered. For Executive Search: describe the process stages (scoping, research, assessment, placement).

✦ Results — what to write

Describe what actually happened as a result of Whitewater's involvement. Bullet form, 3–5 points. Lead with the most concrete outcome.

Include: timelines met or exceeded, quality of outcomes, downstream impact where you know it. For search: appointment date, candidate quality, process quality.

Stat callouts (the dark boxes with large numbers) show the client's revenue and headcount — these are internal only and hidden in client-facing mode. Fill them in when you have this data — they help IEs contextualise the mandate's scale.

Avoid self-congratulatory language like "Whitewater delivered exceptional results." Let the specifics speak.
